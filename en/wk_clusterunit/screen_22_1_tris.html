1.7 Place of Composition

Datatype: GeoNames

CA element code: rapprrec_place_GEO

Quicktip: Linked Data (Geonames) field to indicate the geographical location where the work was recorded or performed. Enter the name of a city, in any language (e.g., Riva presso Chieri, Sulaymaniyah or سلێمانی , Beijing or 北京市, Tehran or تهران, ) and choose from the suggestions in the database. For more precise searches the Geonames codes can be used: ADM1 (first-order administrative division (for Italy, Region - the ADM1 Italy search returns all Italian regions - for the United States: the States - the search ); ADM2 (second-order administrative division, e.g. ADM2 Turin the province of Turin), ADM3 Turin (the city of Turin). The coordinates point to the geographic center of the identified area. For historical places the suffix h can be used, e.g.: ADM2H, Carbonia-Iglesias (former Italian province); PCLH (historical political entity, e.g., PCLH, German Democratic Republic; The use of location codes allows the definition of multiple areas (of physical geography and informal territories, e.g., STNB, scientific research base (STNB McMurdo Station, Antarctica), TERR, territory (TERR Western Sahara); GYSR, geyser, GYSR Géiser del Tatio, Chile, SA. For other codes conultare the documentation.

Datatype: Container

CA element code: instrumentation_CN

Quicktip: Structured container that collects information about the planned staffing for the performance of the work, either in summary (discursive) or analytical (structured and coded) form. The data can be useful for indexing, identification of the work, and comparison between versions.

Usage examples: {to be completed}

1.10.1 Intrumentation – summary

Datatype: Text

CA element code: organico_sintetico

Quicktip: Summary description of the ensemble processed, according to SBN MARC conventions (U9B.2 - M8B2). Contain only the number of solo voices/instruments (followed by "V" or "str") and/or the name of the ensemble (e.g., "2str," "Choir(4V)"). Use coded abbreviations, separated by comma, without spaces, in accordance with the SBN Music guide (U9B.2 - M8B2)

Usage examples: {to be completed}

1.10.2 Intrumentation – detailed (SBN MARC)

Datatype: Text

CA element code: organico_analitico

Quicktip: Analytical detail of the processed staff, according to SBN MARC conventions (U9B.3 - M8B3). Includes each individual voice or instrument with coded abbreviations (e.g., "pf-solo,2fl,2ob"), separated by comma, no spaces. Refer to the SBN Music guide (U9B.3 - M8B3) for schema and allowed codes

1.11.4 suffix

Datatype: List

CA element code: suffix_ME

Quicktip: Optional field for adding qualifiers or suffixes that further specify the medium of performance. Suffixes serve to refine the description of the ensemble or performance, indicating particular characteristics of the performance such as: One-handed performances (one hand) Performances shared by several musicians on one instrument (two players on one instrument) Compositions for several hands (three hands, four hands, six hands, eight hands) Prepared instrument (prepared) Recorded (recorded) Amplified These suffixes are used to semantically enrich the "medium of performance" field, without altering its main structure, but adding specific performance information, which is essential for indexing and analytical cataloging of contemporary or experimental music.
